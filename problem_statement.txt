“SecureWealth Twin” – Intelligent Wealth Growth with Built-in Fraud 
Protection 
Banks today face a dual responsibility: 
 
help customers grow their wealth intelligently and  
 
protect that wealth from fraud, misuse, and risky actions. 
Customers increasingly rely on digital channels for savings, investments, and 
financial planning. While AI-powered wealth advisory can guide users toward 
better financial outcomes, the same digital convenience also exposes them to 
risks such as social-engineering fraud, coerced transactions, OTP misuse, 
and impulsive high-value actions. 
The Bank aims to create “SecureWealth Twin”, an AI-powered digital wealth 
intelligence system that acts as a virtual financial twin of a customer or 
business, while embedding a mandatory, lightweight cyber-security and fraud 
protection layer around all critical wealth-related actions. 
This application should be able to- 
1. Learn from spending, saving, and investment habits 
2. Understand changes in income, goals, and risk appetite 
3. Study market and economic trends (stocks, funds, interest rates, 
inflation, international events and global markets) to give strategic 
recommendations (e.g., sell gold, shift to FD based on global 
indicators) 
4. Integrate with Account Aggregator to access financial information from 
other banks and gather a full financial picture. 
5. Allow customers to add property, gold, vehicles, and other assets to 
compute full net worth. 
6. Give helpful and timely suggestions like - 
 
How to create a portfolio 
 
How much to save every month 
 
When to invest or rebalance a portfolio 
 
How to reduce financial risks 
 
How to reach goals faster (education, home, retirement, etc.) 

Smart tax-saving options

7. Protect wealth actions through built-in risk checks before execution, 
without complicating the user experience. 
 
The system must ensure that wealth is not only created but also 
safeguarded, especially during high-value or sensitive financial actions.
4. Guidelines 
What the Students Are Required to Do 
Students are required to design and demonstrate a single, unified system 
that combines: 
1. Digital Wealth Intelligence 
For Individual Customers (Retail Wealth Management): 
 
Help users build healthy saving habits 
 
Provide goal-based investment guidance (education, home, 
retirement, etc.) 
 
Suggest SIPs, deposits, and simple tax-saving options 
 
Identify overspending and financial risks 
 
Simulate future wealth outcomes 
For Corporate / Business Customers (Optional): 
 
Analyze cash flows and liquidity 
 
Suggest options for surplus fund management 
 
Highlight financial risk indicators 
 
Support long-term growth planning 
2. Mandatory Wealth Protection Layer (Cyber-Security & 
Fraud) 
All critical wealth actions (e.g., starting/modifying SIPs, large 
investments, portfolio rebalancing, surplus fund movement) must pass 
through a mandatory cyber-protection layer that: 
 
Evaluates basic risk signals such as: 
o 
Trusted vs. New device (simulated) 
o 
OTP usage patterns 
o 
Transaction amount vs. historical behavior 
o 
Speed, retries, or urgency of actions 
 
Generates a Wealth Protection Risk Score (Low / Medium / High) 
 
Takes proportionate action: 
o 
Allow action 
o 
Display warning or cooling-off message 
o 
Temporarily block or delay the action

7. Compliance 
Requirements 
1. Customer Data Privacy & Consent 
 
• Always take clear permission from users before using their financial 
data. 
 
• Explain what data you’re collecting and why. 
 
• No hidden data usage! 
 
2. Secure Handling of Financial Information 
  • Use encryption, secure APIs, and avoid storing sensitive data in plain 
text. 
   • Ensure your prototype shows safe data flow (even if simulated). 
 
3. Transparent & Explainable AI 
   • Your AI/logic must not give “mysterious” investment advice. 
   • Show how and why a recommendation was made (simple rules, or logic).
 
4. No Guaranteed Returns or Misleading Claims 
  • Your system should not promise profits or claim “zero risk” investments.

   • Only provide suggestions or simulated outcomes, not final financial 
advice. 
 
5. Follow Basic Financial Regulations 
   • Keep in mind simple rules: 
   • KYC is needed before real investment. 
   • Students can show “for simulation/demo only” in their projects.
